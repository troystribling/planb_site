RSpec is a Ruby Behavior Driven Development DSL. In this article methods of extending the functionality of RSpec will be described. This includes adding both custom matchers and methods within the scope of RSpec describe blocks. Both Brasten Sager and Brandon Keepers describe how to add custom matchers by extending Spec::Rails::Matchers. Neither described their configuration, RSpec gem or plugin, nor exactly what was done to place the matchers within the scope of the describe block. Also, this extension method will only be applicable when the the RSpec Rails plugin is used. The method of adding custom matchers described here will not have a dependency on Spec::Rails::Matchers. The basis of the method used in this article is derived from that of Nick Sieger for an older version of RSpec. For newer versions of RSpec the names of the modules and classes have changed but the strategy is the same.
